A legal document to sue Facebook would include the following:
* The names of the plaintiff(s) and the defendant(s).
* The court in which the case is being filed.
* A statement of the facts of the case.
* A list of the legal claims that are being made.
* A demand for relief.
* A signature of the plaintiff(s).
Here is an example of a legal document to sue Facebook:
John Smith,
Plaintiff,
v.
Facebook, Inc.,
Defendant.
Case No.: 123456789
United States District Court
for the Northern District of California
Plaintiff John Smith hereby files this Complaint against Defendant Facebook, Inc., and alleges as follows:
1. Plaintiff is an individual who resides in California.
2. Defendant is a corporation organized and existing under the laws of Delaware with its principal place of business in California.
3. At all times relevant to this Complaint, Defendant has operated a social media platform known as Facebook.
4. Facebook has collected and stored Plaintiff's personal information, including his name, date of birth, email address, and phone number.
5. Facebook has used Plaintiff's personal information for its own commercial purposes, including selling it to third-party advertisers.
6. Facebook has failed to take reasonable security measures to protect Plaintiff's personal information from unauthorized access, use, or disclosure.
7. As a result of Defendant's negligence, Plaintiff's personal information has been hacked and disclosed to third-party individuals.
8. Plaintiff has suffered damages as a result of Defendant's negligence, including but not limited to:
(a) Emotional distress;
(b) Loss of privacy; and
(c) Damage to his reputation.
WHEREFORE, Plaintiff prays for judgment against Defendant as follows:
1. For compensatory damages in an amount to be proven at trial;
2. For punitive damages;
3. For injunctive relief; and
4. For such other and further relief as the Court may deem just and proper.
Dated: February 25, 2023
John Smith
By:
His Attorney
